Colophon

I build software — and I write about what it teaches me.

I’m a software engineer with more than 17 years shipping web systems: real projects with real deadlines, and the scars to show for it. Over the years, I’ve built solutions from the ground up, improved complex systems, solved production issues, and helped other developers turn hard technical lessons into practical understanding.

The short version

My day job is designing and building web platforms — the last seven years in .NET, C#, Angular and SQL Server, the years before that a mix of whatever the project called for. I've been the junior, the lead, the consultant parachuted in, and the one writing the post-mortem.

My nights and weekends go into writing. The bet is simple: if I can explain a thing clearly to a beginner, I probably understand it. If I can't, I don't. Articles are how I check.

Why this site exists

There's a lot of writing about software that's optimized for going viral: hot takes, tool reviews, breathless framework comparisons. I wanted a quieter place — one where I can sit with a problem for a few thousand words and not apologize for it. This is that place.

It's also where the courses live. Over the past 17+ years I've often helped developers understand software engineering concepts, improve code quality, optimize performance, and identify software bottlenecks. The curriculum here is what I wish I had when I started.
